# Global Macro Research Reports (2025 Q4)

## 1. Global Economic Outlook: Soft Landing Scenarios
**Date:** 2025-10-15
**Author:** Chief Economist, Global Insight
**Summary:**
As inflation metrics stabilize near the 2.5% target across G7 nations, the probability of a "soft landing" has increased to 65%. Labor markets show resilience, though wage growth has moderated. Central banks are expected to pause rate hikes, with potential cuts starting in Q1 2026.

**Key Indicators:**
- **GDP Growth:** Projected at 2.1% globally for 2025.
- **Inflation (CPI):** Trending towards 2.8% YoY.
- **Unemployment:** Stable at 4.2% in US, slight uptick in Eurozone.

**Risks:**
- Geopolitical tensions in Eastern Europe affecting energy prices.
- Supply chain disruptions from new trade tariffs.

## 2. Federal Reserve Policy Update: The Pivot Point
**Date:** 2025-11-02
**Category:** Monetary Policy
**Analysis:**
The latest FOMC minutes suggest a unanimous consensus on maintaining current rates through December. The "higher for longer" narrative is shifting towards "maintenance for stability". Bond markets have priced in a 25bps cut by March 2026.

**Implications:**
- **Treasury Yields:** 10-year yield expected to hover around 3.8-4.0%.
- **USD Strength:** Dollar index (DXY) likely to soften against emerging market currencies.

## 3. Emerging Markets: Opportunities in Southeast Asia
**Date:** 2025-09-28
**Region:** APAC
**Highlights:**
Vietnam and Indonesia continue to benefit from supply chain diversification (China+1 strategy). FDI inflows have increased by 15% YoY. Tech manufacturing and green energy sectors are leading growth.

**Watchlist:**
- Currency volatility in Latin America due to election cycles.
- India's manufacturing PMI expansion.

## 4. Commodity Supercycle Review: Energy & Metals
**Date:** 2025-10-20
**Sector:** Commodities
**Outlook:**
- **Oil:** Brent crude stabilizing at $85/bbl due to OPEC+ supply discipline.
- **Copper:** Demand surge from EV production causing supply deficits; price target revised upward to $9,500/tonne.
- **Gold:** Remains a safe haven hedge amidst lingering geopolitical uncertainty, trading in the $2,400 range.
